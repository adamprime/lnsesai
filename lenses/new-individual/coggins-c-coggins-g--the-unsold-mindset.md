# The Unsold Mindset
**Author:** Coggins, C. & Coggins, G.

**Tags:** SELLING, nan

## Summary

"The Unsold Mindset: Redefining What It Means to Sell" by Colin Coggins and Garrett Brown presents a revolutionary approach to sales that challenges traditional methodologies and stereotypes. The book argues that the most successful salespeople aren't defined by their techniques or tactics, but by their fundamental mindset and way of thinking about the sales process.

The central premise of the book is that conventional sales wisdom has created a toxic culture of manipulation, pressure, and inauthenicity that actually hinders success. Instead of focusing on closing techniques, objection handling, and aggressive pursuit tactics, Coggins and Brown advocate for what they call the "unsold mindset" – an approach that prioritizes authenticity, genuine relationship-building, and serving the customer's best interests above all else.

The authors distinguish between being "sold" (trapped in outdated sales paradigms) and being "unsold" (free from limiting beliefs about what selling should look like). Those with an unsold mindset don't see themselves as traditional salespeople trying to convince or persuade. Instead, they view themselves as problem-solvers, consultants, and trusted advisors who happen to have solutions that can genuinely help their prospects.

Key concepts in the book include the importance of detachment from outcomes, which paradoxically leads to better results. When salespeople become too attached to making a sale, they often exhibit desperate behaviors that repel prospects. The unsold mindset embraces the possibility that a prospect might not buy, and this detachment creates space for authentic conversations and genuine connections.

The book emphasizes that authenticity is not just a nice-to-have quality but a competitive advantage. In an era where buyers are more informed and skeptical than ever, authentic salespeople stand out dramatically from those using manipulative tactics. This authenticity manifests in being honest about product limitations, genuinely caring about whether the solution is right for the prospect, and being willing to walk away from deals that aren't mutually beneficial.

Another crucial framework is the concept of "selling without selling" – creating value and building relationships in ways that don't feel like traditional sales interactions. This might involve sharing insights, making introductions, or providing helpful resources without expecting immediate reciprocation. The unsold mindset recognizes that the best sales often don't feel like sales at all.

The authors also address the psychological barriers that prevent people from embracing sales roles or excelling in them. Many people have negative associations with selling because they equate it with being pushy, manipulative, or inauthentic. The unsold mindset reframes selling as a service profession where success comes from genuinely helping others solve problems.

The book provides practical guidance on how to develop this mindset, including techniques for active listening, asking better questions, and creating consultative conversations rather than sales presentations. It emphasizes the importance of curiosity over persuasion, suggesting that the best salespeople are genuinely interested in understanding their prospects' situations rather than immediately jumping to solutions.

The significance of this book lies in its timing and approach. As traditional sales methods become less effective in our information-rich, relationship-driven economy, "The Unsold Mindset" offers a path forward that aligns with modern buyer expectations and preferences. It's particularly relevant for professionals who need to sell but don't consider themselves salespeople – entrepreneurs, consultants, and service providers who must attract and retain clients.

The book matters because it addresses the fundamental disconnect between how selling is traditionally taught and how it actually works in practice. By focusing on mindset rather than tactics, it provides a sustainable foundation for long-term success that doesn't depend on manipulative techniques or high-pressure approaches that burn out both salespeople and their prospects.

## Theme Analysis

### Theme 1: Authenticity Over Manipulation
**Explanation:** The book argues that genuine authenticity is more powerful than any sales technique or manipulative tactic. Authentic salespeople build trust faster and create longer-lasting relationships because prospects can sense their genuine intentions.
**Examples:** The authors contrast traditional "fake it till you make it" approaches with being honest about product limitations and genuinely caring whether the solution fits the prospect's needs.

### Theme 2: Detachment from Outcomes
**Explanation:** Paradoxically, the less attached salespeople are to making a specific sale, the more successful they become. This detachment allows for more natural conversations and reduces the desperate energy that repels prospects.
**Examples:** The book illustrates how top performers are willing to disqualify prospects and walk away from deals that aren't mutually beneficial, which actually increases their overall success rate.

### Theme 3: Mindset Over Methodology
**Explanation:** The authors emphasize that successful selling is primarily about how you think rather than what specific techniques you use. The right mindset naturally leads to effective behaviors and approaches.
**Examples:** The book shows how salespeople with the unsold mindset naturally ask better questions and listen more effectively because they're genuinely curious rather than just following a script.

### Theme 4: Service-Oriented Selling
**Explanation:** The unsold mindset reframes selling as a service profession where the primary goal is helping prospects solve problems rather than pushing products. This service orientation creates better outcomes for everyone involved.
**Examples:** Examples include sharing valuable insights without expecting immediate reciprocation and making introductions that benefit prospects even when they don't lead to sales.

### Theme 5: Redefining Sales Identity
**Explanation:** Many people resist sales roles because of negative stereotypes about salespeople. The book helps readers develop a positive sales identity based on problem-solving and relationship-building rather than manipulation.
**Examples:** The authors address how professionals in various fields can embrace their sales responsibilities by viewing themselves as consultants and advisors rather than traditional salespeople.

### Theme 6: Curiosity Over Persuasion
**Explanation:** Instead of trying to convince prospects to buy, the unsold mindset emphasizes genuine curiosity about prospects' situations and challenges. This curiosity leads to better solutions and stronger relationships.
**Examples:** The book contrasts traditional sales presentations with consultative conversations driven by thoughtful questions and active listening.
