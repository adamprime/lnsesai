# HR Competencies
**Author:** Brockbank

**Tags:** Management, OD HR

## Summary

"HR from the Outside In: Six Competencies for the Future of Human Resources" by Dave Ulrich, Jon Younger, Wayne Brockbank, and Mike Ulrich represents a paradigm shift in how HR professionals should approach their role in modern organizations. The book challenges the traditional inside-out perspective of HR, where practitioners focus primarily on internal processes and administrative functions, and instead advocates for an outside-in approach that begins with understanding external stakeholders and business context.

The central thesis argues that HR's evolution from administrative function to strategic business partner requires mastering six core competencies that enable professionals to create value for all stakeholders. These competencies are grounded in extensive research involving thousands of HR professionals and business leaders worldwide, providing empirical foundation for the authors' recommendations.

The six competencies framework includes Strategic Positioner, which involves understanding the business context and translating business strategy into HR priorities; Credible Activist, combining personal credibility with the courage to challenge and influence; Capability Builder, focusing on creating organizational capabilities that drive performance; Change Champion, leading and facilitating organizational transformation; Human Resource Innovator, designing and delivering innovative HR practices; and Technology Proponent, leveraging technology to improve HR effectiveness and efficiency.

A key innovation in this work is the emphasis on starting with stakeholder needs rather than HR processes. The authors argue that effective HR professionals must first understand what customers, investors, communities, and employees expect from the organization, then work backward to determine how HR can contribute to meeting those expectations. This outside-in perspective fundamentally reframes HR's role from service provider to value creator.

The book provides practical tools and assessments for developing each competency, including self-evaluation instruments, development planning templates, and case studies demonstrating successful application. The authors emphasize that these competencies are not sequential but interconnected, requiring HR professionals to develop proficiency across all six areas while recognizing individual strengths and development needs.

The research methodology underlying the book involved surveying over 20,000 HR professionals and business leaders across multiple industries and geographies, making it one of the most comprehensive studies of HR effectiveness ever conducted. This empirical foundation distinguishes the work from purely theoretical or anecdotal approaches to HR development.

The authors also address the changing business environment that necessitates this competency evolution, including globalization, technological advancement, demographic shifts, and increasing stakeholder expectations. They argue that HR professionals who master these competencies will be better positioned to navigate complexity and drive organizational success in an increasingly dynamic business landscape.

The book's significance extends beyond individual professional development to organizational transformation. By developing these competencies, HR professionals can elevate their function's strategic contribution, improve business outcomes, and enhance stakeholder value. The authors provide evidence that organizations with competent HR professionals achieve better financial performance, higher employee engagement, and stronger customer relationships.

## Theme Analysis

### Theme 1: Outside-In Perspective
**Explanation:** The fundamental shift from focusing on internal HR processes to understanding external stakeholder needs first. This approach ensures HR activities create genuine value for customers, investors, and communities rather than just internal efficiency.
**Examples:** Starting strategic planning by analyzing customer expectations rather than internal capabilities; designing employee experiences that reflect customer service standards; aligning talent development with market demands.

### Theme 2: Strategic Business Partnership
**Explanation:** HR's evolution from administrative support to strategic contributor requires deep business acumen and the ability to translate business strategy into people practices. This theme emphasizes HR's role in driving organizational performance.
**Examples:** HR professionals participating in strategic planning sessions; translating market opportunities into talent requirements; measuring HR impact through business metrics rather than activity measures.

### Theme 3: Competency-Based Development
**Explanation:** The six competencies provide a comprehensive framework for HR professional development, moving beyond technical skills to include business understanding, relationship building, and change leadership capabilities.
**Examples:** Self-assessment tools for each competency; development planning processes; career progression models based on competency mastery rather than tenure or technical expertise.

### Theme 4: Technology Integration
**Explanation:** Modern HR effectiveness requires leveraging technology not just for efficiency but for enhanced decision-making, employee experience, and strategic insight. Technology becomes an enabler of HR transformation rather than just a tool.
**Examples:** Using analytics for workforce planning; implementing digital platforms for employee engagement; leveraging AI for talent acquisition and development.

### Theme 5: Change Leadership
**Explanation:** HR professionals must become change champions who can lead organizational transformation while helping others navigate complexity and uncertainty. This involves both personal change agility and organizational change management skills.
**Examples:** Leading cultural transformation initiatives; facilitating organizational restructuring; developing change capability throughout the organization.

### Theme 6: Stakeholder Value Creation
**Explanation:** Success is measured by the value created for all organizational stakeholders rather than just internal satisfaction. This requires understanding diverse stakeholder needs and balancing competing interests while driving overall organizational success.
**Examples:** Designing programs that enhance both employee experience and customer satisfaction; creating talent strategies that satisfy investor expectations while supporting community development; measuring success through stakeholder value metrics.
