Unlike traditional stress, which we can identify and address, microstresses fly under our psychological radar, making them particularly insidious.

Cross and Dillon demonstrate through extensive research and real-world examples how these small stressors compound exponentially. A brief misalignment with a colleague, an unclear email that requires mental energy to decode, a last-minute priority shift, or a technology glitch may seem trivial individually.
